About me

I’m a Unity-focused game and VR-simulation developer based in Auckland, New Zealand, with more than a decade of professional C# work and a dozen shipped titles across mobile, desktop, and head-mounted displays. Whether that’s a classroom RTS, a surgical-skills trainer, or a bite-size STEM mini-game—and I’m always looking for the delivery format that makes the lesson stick.

I approach problems holistically zooming out to see design intent, technical constraints, and player psychology, then zooming in to craft tight, data-driven systems that play nicely together. I understand the need to put things in front of people quickly and vet how well things translate to reality. I approach creation in a clean modular way where ideas can be quickly surfaced and iterated on in a data driven manner.
